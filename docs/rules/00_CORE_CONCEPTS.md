# Core Concepts

*Draft - Subject to change.*

## The Golden Rule
If a rule is unclear or players disagree on line of sight or measurement, roll a D6. On a 1-3, Player A decides. On a 4-6, Player B decides. Keep the game moving.

## What You Need to Play
- Miniatures (from 1 to around 24 per player).
- Some way to demarcate Zones on your play area (e.g., terrain features, drawn lines on a mat, or string).
- A large handful of six-sided dice (D6s).
- Extra D6s to use as trackers for Wounds and other effects.

## Dice Mechanics & The "Rule of 4" & "Rule of 6"
The game uses six-sided dice (D6). The core resolution system is built around these two rules:
- **The Rule of 4 (Base Success):** Whenever a model takes an action that requires a roll (like attacking), you will roll a pool of D6s. By default, **every die that rolls a 4, 5, or 6 is a Success.** Elite or powerful units do not hit on a lower number; instead, they reflect their prowess by rolling *more* dice in their pool.
- **The Rule of 6 (Maximum Target Value):** Modifiers (like Dodge or Cover) can increase the success number required to hit a model (e.g., from 4+ to 5+ or 6+). However, this success number **can never be modified to be higher than 6+**. A natural roll of a **6** is always a success, ensuring no model is ever mathematically immune to actions.

## Unit Rule Cards & D6 Trackers
Every unit in your army requires a **Rule Card** that displays its Unit Profile and any special rules. Because Bodacious Battles allows players to design custom units, you must bring **two copies** of each unit's rule card:
- One copy for you to reference and use for tracking.
- One copy to hand to your opponent at the start of the game so they can easily reference your custom stats.

To keep the play area clean and free of custom tokens, Bodacious Battles uses D6s as trackers placed directly on your Rule Card, rather than next to the miniature on the table.
- **Action Points (AP):** When a unit activates, place a D6 on its Rule Card showing its total AP (e.g., '3'). As it spends AP to move or shoot, turn the die down. When it reaches '0', the activation ends and the die is removed.
- **Wounds:** Place a D6 of a different color on the unit's Rule Card to track its remaining Wounds. As it takes damage, tick the die down. When it reaches 0, the model is destroyed.

## Movement & Line of Sight (Zones)
The battlefield is divided into discrete **Zones**. A zone could be a room, a rooftop, a section of forest, or an open street.

Models move between adjacent zones rather than measuring inches. Line of Sight (LoS) and Cover are determined by the relationship between zones (e.g., Zone A can see Zone B, and Zone C provides cover). This eliminates the need for tape measures and arguments over true-line-of-sight.

## Unit Profiles
Every model in the game is defined by a Unit Profile, which dictates its capabilities on the battlefield. A standard profile includes:

- **Action Points (AP):** The energy the model spends to move and act during its activation (usually 4 to 6).
- **Combat (CMB):** The base number of D6s the model rolls when attacking. (Specific weapons may modify this).
- **Wounds (W):** How much damage the model can sustain before being destroyed.
- **Dodge:** Increases the Target Number required to hit this model. (e.g., Dodge 1 means attacks hit on a 5+ instead of a 4+).
- **Parry:** Subtracts a flat number of successful hits from incoming Melee attacks.
- **Armor:** A divider for incoming damage. Total Wounds taken = `Floor(Successful Hits / Armor)`.
